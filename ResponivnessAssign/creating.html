Rhythm Beat Mobility

Rhythm games are more of a timing synced beat the user needs to stick with to complete the level. Synced to the music and timing to click or tap the screen as well as holding left click on the mouse or holding on the screen to fly or things like that. A game to talk about when it comes to this is Geometry Dash, a mobile and PC game on both Steam and Google Play (Google is trying to take it down though)

Geometry Dash is a video game where players need to fly, switch gravities, and jump their way through spikes, blocks, and monsters to not crash in the level they are playing, either it is from the creator of Geometry Dash or not. When I say that it isn't always from the creator of the game, Robtop, I mean it. There are online levels to play and run trough as well. As long as players doesn't get the Lite Version (Demo) of Geometry Dash, players are allowed to get different skins, colors, trails, and death effects. There is also more secrets in the full version of Geometry Dash that only costs about 3 dollars. This level is called Theory Of Everything or in short, ToE.

I also have never mention the difficulty ratings shown by the face next to the level. Theory of Everythiing is a insane rating meaning it is very hard to try and complete the level but not too impossible. During Geoemtry Dash's earlier days, Insane was seen as the hardest difficulty and a few levels that once was rated insane is now rated harder. But later on, a new difficulty would be created. This difficulty give the most rewards and viewed more harder than a insane rating. This was the next step up with levels being made then to now.
